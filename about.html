About

In so few words

I study Computer Science and spend most of my time learning how systems work and how to make them better. When I am not studying, I build small tools, experiment with web design, and read. I enjoy teaching and mentoring, whether that means helping friends debug code or explaining algorithms in simple terms.

See my work on GitHub, connect on LinkedIn, or view my résumé.

In more than a few words

I was born in Karachi, Pakistan, and spent part of my childhood there before moving several times. My family lived in Dubai for a few years, then settled in Calgary. Frequent change taught me how to adapt quickly and find comfort in starting over. That is likely why I enjoy building things from scratch and understanding how they work beneath the surface.

I have always been curious about structure and design. I like seeing how lines of code form patterns and how those patterns grow into systems. That curiosity led me to software: writing programs, testing ideas, and refining them until they work the way they should.

My academic interests revolve around systems and data. I am drawn to how things connect, scale, and communicate. I am also interested in engineering design and product strategy, especially when technology simplifies everyday work.

Outside of academics, I read both fiction and non-fiction, keep short notes, and explore topics in design and history. I admire people who create quietly and value consistency more than attention.
