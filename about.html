In so few words

I study Computer Science and enjoy learning how systems work and how thoughtful design can improve them. Outside of classes I build small tools, read, and write about ideas that connect technology and people.

I was born in Lahore, spent part of my childhood in Al Ain, and later moved to Calgary. Moving often taught me to adapt easily and find comfort in change.

I am curious about systems, design, and the small details that shape structure. I like seeing how lines of code form patterns and how those patterns evolve into systems. That curiosity led me to programming, testing, and refining until things feel right.

Somewhere beyond

Outside of computing, I have always been drawn to space. I find a sense of calm and awe in learning about the universe. I often read about general relativity and special relativity, and how these ideas shape our understanding of time, light, and gravity.

I am especially interested in black holes such as Sagittarius A*, Messier 87*, and Cygnus X-1. They represent some of nature’s most fascinating contradictions, where simplicity and complexity meet in perfect balance.

On clear nights, I like looking up at the stars. It reminds me how small we are compared to everything else, and how lucky we are to even be able to look at it.

When I am not coding or reading about the universe, I spend time learning about art, design, and the ways people create meaningful work.

Everything good is built slowly.
